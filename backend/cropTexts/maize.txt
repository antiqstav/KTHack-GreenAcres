Maize
Maize, or corn, is a versatile cereal crop grown for food, fodder, and industrial uses, thriving in sunny, warm climates.

How to plant it:

Sow seeds 1-2 inches deep and space them 8-10 inches apart.
Maintain a row spacing of 24-30 inches for optimal growth.
Best practices:

Apply nitrogen-based fertilizers during the growing phase.
Ensure adequate sunlight and water supply.
Monitor for pests like stem borers and apply control measures.
