Pigeon Peas
Pigeon peas are a legume crop grown for their protein-rich seeds, adaptable to warm climates and poor soils.

How to plant it:

Sow seeds 1-2 inches deep and 6-12 inches apart.
Maintain rows spaced 18-24 inches apart.
Best practices:

Use phosphorus-rich fertilizers for better root development.
Monitor for pod borers and wilt diseases.
Intercrop with cereals for better soil utilization.
