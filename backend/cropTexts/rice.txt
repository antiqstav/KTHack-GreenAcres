Rice
Rice is a staple cereal crop cultivated in flooded or irrigated fields, flourishing in warm, humid climates.

How to plant it:

Transplant young seedlings into puddled fields at 6-8 inches apart.
Maintain a row spacing of 9-12 inches for uniform growth.
Best practices:

Use nitrogen-rich fertilizers during vegetative stages.
Ensure consistent water coverage in the fields.
Control pests like rice borers and diseases like blast.
