Cotton
Cotton is a major cash crop used in textiles, thriving in warm, well-drained soil and requiring ample sunlight.

How to plant it:

Sow seeds 1-2 inches deep in rows spaced 30-40 inches apart.
Plant during late spring or early summer.
Best practices:

Use certified seeds resistant to pests like bollworms.
Apply nitrogen-based fertilizers to encourage growth.
Practice crop rotation to prevent pest buildup.
